India's Water Body Crisis

India's water bodies are facing unprecedented threats from pollution, encroachment, and climate change. Over 70% of surface water in India is contaminated, affecting millions of people and ecosystems.

  • Pollution: Industrial discharge, sewage, and agricultural runoff contaminating water sources
  • Encroachment: Illegal construction reducing water body area and capacity
  • Silting: Reduced water holding capacity due to sedimentation
  • Biodiversity Loss: Aquatic ecosystems collapsing due to habitat degradation

India has lost over 30% of its water bodies in the last 40 years due to urbanization and pollution

Advanced Restoration Technologies

Bioremediation

Using specific microorganisms and plants to naturally break down pollutants and improve water quality

Aeration Systems

Installing oxygen infusion systems to improve dissolved oxygen levels and support aquatic life

Phytoremediation

Using specific aquatic plants to absorb heavy metals and nutrients from contaminated water

Comprehensive Restoration Solutions

Tailored approaches for different types of water bodies and degradation levels.

Urban Lake Restoration

Reviving polluted and encroached urban lakes through desilting, bioremediation, and shoreline development.

  • Scientific desilting
  • Sewage diversion
  • Shoreline stabilization

River Rejuvenation

Restoring river ecosystems through pollution control, flow management, and riparian zone development.

  • Pollution source identification
  • Riparian buffer creation
  • In-stream habitat improvement

Wetland Restoration

Reviving degraded wetlands through hydrological restoration, native species plantation, and biodiversity enhancement.

  • Hydrological assessment
  • Native species reintroduction
  • Community-based management
