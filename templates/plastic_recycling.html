The Plastic Problem in India

India generates approximately 3.4 million tons of plastic waste annually, with only a fraction being properly recycled. The rest ends up in landfills, water bodies, or is burned, causing severe environmental and health issues.

Environmental Damage

Plastic pollution harms wildlife, clogs waterways, and contributes to microplastic contamination in soil and water.

Health Impacts

Burning plastic releases toxic chemicals, while microplastics enter the food chain, posing health risks.

Economic Costs

Municipalities spend significant resources on waste management, while valuable materials are lost to landfills.

The Plastic Recycling Process

Our comprehensive recycling process transforms plastic waste into valuable resources through a systematic approach that ensures maximum recovery and minimal environmental impact.

Collection & Segregation

Plastic waste is collected from households, businesses, and collection centers. It is then segregated by type and color to ensure efficient processing.

Household Collection Drop-off Centers Commercial Waste

Sorting & Washing

Plastics are further sorted by polymer type using manual and automated methods. They are then thoroughly washed to remove contaminants like labels, adhesives, and residues.

Manual Sorting Automated Sorting Contaminant Removal

Shredding & Granulating

Clean plastics are shredded into small flakes or granules, making them easier to process and ensuring uniform melting in subsequent steps.

Flake Production Granule Formation Size Reduction

Melting & Pelletizing

Plastic flakes are melted and extruded through fine screens to remove any remaining impurities. The molten plastic is then formed into uniform pellets for manufacturing.

Extrusion Filtration Pellet Formation

Manufacturing New Products

Recycled plastic pellets are used to manufacture new products, completing the circular economy loop and reducing the need for virgin plastic materials.

Packaging Construction Materials Textiles Consumer Goods

Types of Plastic We Recycle

We process various types of plastics, each with unique properties and recycling methods. Proper identification is key to effective recycling.

PET

Polyethylene Terephthalate

Recycling Code: 1

Commonly used in beverage bottles, food containers, and polyester fibers. Highly recyclable with good market value.

Common Products: Water bottles, soda bottles, food jars
HDPE

High-Density Polyethylene

Recycling Code: 2

Used in milk jugs, detergent bottles, and plastic bags. Durable and widely recycled into new containers and plastic lumber.

Common Products: Milk jugs, shampoo bottles, detergent containers
PVC

Polyvinyl Chloride

Recycling Code: 3

Found in pipes, window frames, and credit cards. Challenging to recycle due to chlorine content but we have specialized processes.

Common Products: Pipes, window frames, blister packaging
LDPE

Low-Density Polyethylene

Recycling Code: 4

Used in plastic bags, squeeze bottles, and food wrap. Flexible and commonly recycled into new bags and composite lumber.

Common Products: Plastic bags, cling film, squeeze bottles
PP

Polypropylene

Recycling Code: 5

Found in yogurt containers, bottle caps, and prescription bottles. Heat resistant and increasingly recycled.

Common Products: Yogurt containers, bottle caps, medicine bottles
PS

Polystyrene

Recycling Code: 6

Used in disposable cups, packaging materials, and insulation. Lightweight but challenging to recycle economically.

Common Products: Disposable cups, food containers, packaging foam
