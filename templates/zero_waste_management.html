India's Waste Management Challenge

India generates over 62 million tonnes of municipal solid waste annually, with only 70% collected and less than 20% processed. This leads to:

  • Landfill Overload: Overflowing landfills polluting air, water, and soil
  • Greenhouse Gas Emissions: Methane from decomposing waste contributes to climate change
  • Resource Depletion: Valuable materials lost instead of being recycled
  • Public Health Risks: Improper waste management spreads diseases

By 2030, India's waste generation is expected to reach 165 million tonnes annually without intervention

The Zero Waste Hierarchy

Redesign systems to eliminate waste generation at source

Reduce & Reuse

Minimize waste and extend product lifecycles

Recycle & Compost

Recover

Extract energy from non-recyclable materials

Residuals
